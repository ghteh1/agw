01 / Product

Biochar

Pyrolysis · Circular by-product

Biochar is a carbon residue derived from waste biomass through pyrolysis — heating at high temperature with limited oxygen. Produced alongside clean energy at our syngas and biochar facility, it turns wood waste into a high-value, carbon-negative material with applications across construction and agriculture.

Carbon-negative — locks captured carbon into a stable form that can be stored for over 1,000 years.

From our own wood waste — a circular by-product of our energy generation, with zero residue to landfill.

Dual application — engineered for both civil infrastructure and high-yield sustainable agriculture.

Carbon Negative Pyrolysis Circular By-product
Function 01 · Civil

Road Base & Concrete Applications

By integrating biochar into road bases and concrete, we build stronger, more durable infrastructure with a significantly lower carbon footprint. It enhances road stability by improving load distribution and reducing moisture damage, and reinforces concrete structures without affecting setting times. Replacing traditional fillers with biochar (typically 0.5–5% by weight) delivers high-performance civil engineering solutions that are as resilient as they are responsible.

Function 02 · Agriculture

Fertiliser & Soil Application Performance

Biochar acts as a powerful “nutrient sponge” that boosts fertiliser efficiency while protecting the environment. Blending just 2–5% with NPK fertilisers can reduce total fertiliser usage by 20–30%, locking in nutrients and releasing them slowly to plants. Fully compatible with organic, mineral and liquid fertilisers, it improves soil structure and stores carbon for over 1,000 years — a definitive solution for high-yield, sustainable agriculture.
